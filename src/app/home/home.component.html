What is UMSL|HACK?


UMSL|HACK is an annual 36 hour hackathon hosted by the Information Systems Department at the University of Missouri-St. Louis. UMSL|HACK main goal is to create a platform to challenge/encourage college students to apply thier skills by building an application in 36 hours! We know how valuable hackathons are to college students, while it might be intimidating to have a running applicaiton in 36 hours, we work very hard to provide participants with all the mentoring and help they need to get their app running. Our end goal is to make sure that all of our participants have learned something new in those 36 hours! We hope by particaping in UMSL|HACK students will gain valuable skills as they will work as a team to complete a specific task that requires a diverse skill set.


UMSL|HACK was established after a group of UMSL students partiacpated at their first ever hackathon and wanted to share the experiance with the UMSL communinty. The first ever UMSL|HACK was March 3rd-5th 2017 and turned out to be a huge success as the winning team Younivise saw their idea incubated into a business with the help of UMSL|Accelerate. After reciving an overwhelming amount of positive feedback and participants asking "When is the next UMSL|HACK?". We have decided to organize another hackathon UMSL|HACK: Got API February 16th-18th.

UMSL|HACK: Got API!


UMSL Hack: Got API! will be held on Friday February 16th from 5:00 PM to Sunday February 18th 6:00 PM at the University of Missouri-St. Louis Express Script Hall. The theme of this event is "GOT API!", where students will work in teams of 3-5 people to create projects focused on creatively by harnessing the power of the API. Our goal is to see how effectively students work with existing data and interfaces to solve real problems much as they would encounter in day-to-day business.
